### The Problem with Tap Water on Glass

Even though it looks clear, tap water often contains **minerals like calcium, magnesium, sodium, and copper**. These microscopic particles stay behind when the water evaporates, leaving behind dull spots and streaks—especially visible on windows.
Mineral content varies by region. For example:

**Western states** often have &#8220;harder&#8221; water with higher mineral content.

**Southern areas** may have moderate to low mineral levels. Regardless of location, untreated tap water will almost always leave behind some kind of residue.

Another issue is **water pH**. If the pH is too high or unbalanced, it can prevent cleaning agents from binding with dirt effectively, making it harder to lift grease or grime off your windows—even if you’re using a quality cleaner.

## How to Purify Water for Window Cleaning

There are several ways to purify tap water at home for better Window Cleaning:

### 🫧 1. **Distilled Water**

**Distilled water** is one of the most accessible and affordable options for homeowners. The process involves heating water until it becomes steam, then collecting and cooling that steam into pure water—leaving minerals behind.
**DIY Distillation at Home:**

Fill a large pot with water and place a smaller heat-safe bowl or pot inside.

Place the lid on the large pot **upside down**, so condensation drips into the smaller bowl.

Add ice to the top of the lid to speed up condensation.

Bring the water to a gentle boil and allow the purified condensation to collect.

### 💧 2. **Reverse Osmosis (RO)**

**Reverse osmosis** filters out minerals by pushing tap water through a semi-permeable membrane. The result is purified water that won’t leave behind any streak-causing particles.
While more of an investment, **home RO systems** are available and can connect directly to your water line for regular use.

### ⚡ 3. **Ionization or Bio-Ceramic Filters**

Ionizing machines and **bio-ceramic filters** use magnetism to neutralize and remove mineral content from water. These systems often raise the alkalinity of the water as well, improving its ability to break down grease and lift dirt from glass without streaking.
While typically more expensive, they can be a long-term solution for homeowners who want to ensure spotless results every time they clean.
