## Why Do Pavers Deteriorate Over Time?

Even though pavers are designed to be tough, they still take a beating from the elements:

**Rain and snow** contribute to erosion and moss growth.

**UV rays** fade the color and finish over time.

**Dirt, oil, and debris** settle in the joints and surface.

**Weeds and moss** grow in the cracks, weakening the structure.

Without proper maintenance, your once-beautiful pavers can start to look dull, dirty, and uneven. Fortunately, a thorough cleaning and sealing process can restore their appearance and help prevent future damage.

## The Paver Restoration Process: Step by Step

### 1. **Deep Surface Cleaning**

First, all dirt, moss, and grime are removed using **professional-grade Pressure Washing equipment**. This step is crucial—it clears the way for restoration products to bond properly and reveals the true condition of the pavers.

Oil stains, algae, and surface buildup are treated with eco-friendly cleaning solutions.

Weeds are cleared from the joints.

The surface is allowed to dry completely before the next step.

### 2. **Re-Sanding the Joints**

After cleaning, the joints between your pavers may have lost their structural sand. Valley Window Care and Exterior Cleaning **reapply polymeric sand** or jointing sand to stabilize the pavers and block weed growth.

This step improves the look and structure of your patio or driveway.

Polymeric sand also helps reduce ant hills and washouts.

### 3. **Sealing the Surface**

The final and most important step is **applying a high-quality paver sealer**. This enhances the color, adds a protective barrier, and prevents stains from setting in.
You can choose from finishes like:

**Natural matte** for a clean, subtle look

**Wet look** for deeper color and shine

**Glossy finish** for bold, vibrant results

Sealing also protects against UV rays, oil stains, water absorption, and erosion.

## How Often Should You Restore Your Pavers?

In general, Valley Window Care and Exterior Cleaning recommend **cleaning and sealing your pavers every 2–3 years**, depending on foot traffic, weather exposure, and the type of sealer used. High-traffic areas like driveways may need more frequent attention, while patios and walkways often hold up longer.
Regular maintenance not only protects your pavers but helps you avoid costly repairs down the road.

## Can You DIY It?

While DIY kits are available, **professional restoration delivers better and longer-lasting results**. Experts know how to:

Choose the right pressure and products

Properly remove old sealer without damaging the pavers

Apply sand and sealer evenly

Handle larger areas efficiently

Plus, you&#8217;ll avoid common mistakes like uneven sealing, over-sanding, or surface damage.
