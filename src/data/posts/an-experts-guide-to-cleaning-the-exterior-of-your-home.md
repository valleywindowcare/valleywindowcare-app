## 1. Prep Your Property for Cleaning

Before breaking out the hose or pressure washer, it’s important to plan around the weather. Choose a day that’s dry and mild—not too hot or cold—so that cleaning solutions work effectively and surfaces dry properly.
Next, clear your workspace:

Move outdoor furniture, grills, and toys out of the way.

Cover plants, electrical outlets, and vents with plastic sheeting and duct tape to protect them.

Have all necessary tools and cleaning products ready to go.

A little preparation goes a long way in making your exterior cleaning smooth, safe, and efficient.

## 2. Start from the Top: Clean Out Your Gutters

Cleaning your gutters may not be glamorous, but it’s a crucial step. Clogged gutters lead to water overflow, which can damage your roof, siding, and foundation.
If you&#8217;re doing it yourself:

Use a sturdy ladder and wear gloves.

Scoop debris out by hand or use a gutter cleaning tool.

Rinse with a garden hose to ensure proper flow.

Prefer to skip the ladder? At Valley Window Care, Valley Window Care and Exterior Cleaning offer **professional gutter cleaning** that removes blockages safely and thoroughly—no risk, no mess.

## 3. Wash Down Your Siding

Your home’s siding is constantly exposed to the elements, so it’s no surprise it collects mildew, mold, pollen, and grime. Not only does this look bad—it can actually damage your home and affect your air quality.[
For general cleaning](https://www.universalwindowsdirect.com/blog/442-how-to-clean-vinyl-siding/):

Use a bucket of warm soapy water (dish soap works well), a soft-bristle brush, and your garden hose.

Scrub the siding gently and rinse from top to bottom.

For tougher jobs, **pressure washing** is highly effective—but only when done correctly. If you’re unsure whether your siding can handle the pressure, or just want a faster, deeper clean, professional services are your safest bet.

## 4. Don’t Forget the Windows

Clean windows dramatically improve your home’s appearance. Plus, removing build-up like mold and dirt protects your window frames and trim from damage.
To get that streak-free shine:

Mix a mild window cleaner or soapy water.

Use a sponge or scrub brush for frames and shutters.

Finish with a squeegee or microfiber cloth on the glass.

Window cleaning is time-consuming, especially for large homes or second stories. If you&#8217;re short on time or want a flawless finish, we offer **professional Window Cleaning** in Green Bay and Appleton that gets the job done right.

## 5. Refresh Your Patio or Deck

Patios and decks are high-traffic areas that gather everything from food stains to moss and mildew. Over time, these surfaces can become slippery or structurally compromised.
Here’s how to clean them:

Use a deck cleaner and stiff-bristle brush to scrub away stains and algae.

Rinse thoroughly with your hose—or better yet, book an annual **deck Pressure Washing** for lasting results.

If you notice dark patches, discoloration, or buildup, it&#8217;s a clear sign it’s time for a deep clean.

## 6. Finish Strong with the Driveway

Your driveway is one of the first things people see—and also one of the most overlooked when it comes to cleaning.
To restore its appearance:

Sweep away debris and loose dirt.

Use a driveway cleaner or degreaser to lift oil stains and grime.

For best results, **pressure wash** the entire surface for a refreshed look and improved safety.

Professional driveway cleaning not only enhances curb appeal but also prevents cracks, weed growth, and surface erosion.
