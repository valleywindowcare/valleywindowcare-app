## 5 Factors That Determine How Often to Clean Your Roof

### 1. Roof Age

The older your roof, the more care it needs. While newer roofs still benefit from cleaning, older roofs (especially those over 10 years) should be cleaned **at least twice a year**. This helps identify issues like cracked shingles, lifted flashing, or signs of water damage before they become serious.
Even if your roof is fairly new, routine cleaning helps preserve its appearance and prevent organic growth from taking hold.

### 2. Roof Color & Pitch

Light-colored roofs show dirt and stains more easily than darker ones. If you have a pale roof, you&#8217;ll likely need to clean it **once or twice a year**, especially in areas prone to heavy rain or snow.
The slope of your roof also plays a role. A steep-pitched roof sheds water and debris more efficiently than a flat or low-slope roof, which allows grime and organic matter to settle and build up over time. Flatter roofs may require more frequent attention.

### 3. Roofing Material

Different roofing materials require different care schedules. For example:

**Asphalt, cedar, or shake shingles**: These typically hide dirt well and may only need cleaning **once per year**.

**Tile and metal roofs**: These materials are prone to mildew stains and can show grime easily, so they may need cleaning every **6 to 8 months**.

Some materials are also more fragile. Asphalt shingles, for instance, can be damaged by high-pressure washing, which strips away their protective granules. That’s why Valley Window Care and Exterior Cleaning use **soft washing methods** that clean effectively without risking damage.

### 4. Climate & Local Conditions

If you live in an area with lots of wind, rain, snow, or tree coverage, your roof is more likely to collect debris, branches, and algae. Homes surrounded by trees, in particular, should be monitored closely for moss and clogged gutters.
These environmental factors contribute to staining, shingle degradation, and blocked drainage systems—all of which can be avoided with consistent roof cleaning.

### 5. Safety & Accessibility

The reality is, roof cleaning isn’t a DIY job for most homeowners. From slippery surfaces to steep slopes, working on a roof can be dangerous without the right tools and training.
Our team at Valley Window Care has the **experience, equipment, and safety measures** in place to clean your roof thoroughly and safely—without damaging the materials.

## Why You Should Never Pressure Wash Your Roof

While Pressure Washing might seem like the fastest way to clean your roof, it can actually do more harm than good. High-pressure water can:

Strip granules off asphalt shingles

Crack tiles or loosen materials

Expose underlayment and cause leaks

Instead, we use a **low-pressure soft washing system** paired with eco-friendly solutions to safely kill mold, mildew, and algae—without compromising your roof’s integrity.
