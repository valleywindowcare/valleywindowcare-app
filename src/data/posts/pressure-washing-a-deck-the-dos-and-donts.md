## The Right Way to Pressure Wash a Deck

Pressure Washing is one of the most effective ways to remove built-up dirt, mildew, and old finish—but only if it’s done carefully.
### Step 1: Prep Your Equipment

Before starting, make sure your pressure washer is set up correctly. If it’s electric, ensure both the water and power sources are secure. For gas machines, double-check the fuel and connection lines. In either case, **make sure water is flowing through the machine before pulling the trigger.**
### Step 2: Apply the Right Pressure

Not all pressure washers are the same. Some machines can push water out at over 4,000 PSI (pounds per square inch)—which is great for concrete, but **way too intense for wood.**To clean your deck without damaging the surface, keep your settings around **1,200–1,500 PSI**. This provides enough pressure to lift dirt and mold **without etching or splintering the boards**.
### Step 3: Maintain a Safe Distance

Getting the right distance from the surface is key. Hold the nozzle **3 to 6 inches away** from the deck and move in smooth, even strokes. Too close and you’ll scar the wood; too far and you won’t get a thorough clean.

### Safety First

Pressure washers are powerful tools. Always wear **protective eyewear** to shield yourself from flying debris. Waterproof, non-slip shoes are also a must, since the surface will get slick as you clean. Safety gear helps prevent slips and keeps you focused on the task at hand.

## Should I Pressure Wash Before Staining My Deck?

Absolutely. Pressure Washing before staining removes the old, weathered top layer of wood, allowing the new stain to absorb evenly. It also eliminates mold, mildew, and built-up grime that would otherwise shorten the life of your finish.
Once you&#8217;ve pressure washed the deck, **give it at least 48 hours to dry** before applying stain. If your deck is shaded or doesn’t get much sun, allow up to 72 hours to ensure it’s completely dry.

## Is Pressure Washing My Deck Worth It?

Yes—when done regularly and correctly, pressure washing your deck can:

Brighten faded wood by removing surface oxidation

Extend the life of your deck stain or sealer

Prevent mold and mildew from setting into the wood

Save you money by reducing the need for premature repairs or board replacement

Boost your curb appeal and outdoor comfort

Ideally, you should schedule a cleaning **once or twice a year**, especially after long winters or before refinishing.
