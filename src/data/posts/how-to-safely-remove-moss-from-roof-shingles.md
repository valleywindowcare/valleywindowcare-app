## Why Moss on Roof Shingles Is a Problem

Moss thrives in damp, shaded environments and spreads quickly across asphalt shingles.
Unchecked moss can:

Lift and separate shingles

Trap moisture against the roof surface

Accelerate shingle deterioration

Cause granule loss

Lead to leaks and premature roof replacement

Removing moss correctly isn’t just about appearance — it’s about **protecting your roof investment**.

## What NOT to Do When Removing Roof Moss

Before explaining the safe method, it’s important to understand what causes damage.
### ❌ Never Pressure Wash a Roof

High-Pressure Washing can:

Strip protective granules

Crack or loosen shingles

Force water under shingles

Void manufacturer warranties

Even low pressure in inexperienced hands can cause irreversible damage.
### ❌ Avoid Scraping or Brushing Aggressively

Using stiff brushes or metal tools can:

Tear shingles

Remove granules

Shorten roof lifespan

### ❌ Do Not Use Harsh DIY Chemicals

Bleach-heavy or unapproved chemicals can:

Damage shingles

Kill landscaping

Create runoff issues

Cause uneven discoloration

## The Safe Way to Remove Moss From Roof Shingles

### ✅ Soft Washing (The Industry-Approved Method)

The safest and most effective way to remove moss from roof shingles is **soft washing**.
Soft washing uses:

**Low water pressure**

**Specialized cleaning solutions**

**Controlled application techniques**

Instead of blasting moss off, soft washing **kills moss at the root**, allowing it to break down and wash away naturally over time.

## Can Homeowners Remove Moss Themselves?

Light moss growth can sometimes be treated with homeowner-applied treatments, but there are risks:

Roof safety hazards (slips and falls)

Improper chemical dilution

Uneven treatment

Missed root systems

Potential shingle damage

For moderate to heavy moss growth, **professional soft washing is strongly recommended**.
