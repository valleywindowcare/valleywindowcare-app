## What Influences Pressure Washing Costs

Several factors cause costs to vary by job:

**Size and complexity of the job** – Larger homes require more hours and water. Two‑story homes cost more than single‑story homes; national data show that a two‑story house can cost **$400–$1 400**, while a single‑story home ranges **$150–$750**.

**Type of surface and grime level** – Power washing (hot water) is more expensive than standard pressure washing. Materials like wood or stucco require gentler soft‑wash techniques that raise per‑square‑foot pricing.

**Location and demand** – Urban areas have higher labor rates. For example, Milwaukee’s averages exceed those in Appleton and Green Bay.

**Season** – Peak demand in spring and summer pushes prices up. Off‑season (late fall) often offers discounts.

**Additional services** – Cleaning decks, driveways, roofs or gutters adds to the total. HomeAdvisor’s national data show driveways cost **$100–$250**, patios **$100–$200**, and roof cleaning **$0.50–$1 per sq ft**.

## Cost per Square Foot, Hour and Service Type

**Per square foot:** National averages range from **$0.35–$0.77 per sq ft** for power washing. Pressure washing (cold water) typically runs **$0.08–$0.20 per sq ft**. Wisconsin commercial rates are around **$0.52–$0.56 per sq ft**.

**Per hour:** Professionals charge **$50–$160 per hour**. Projects requiring specialized equipment or safety gear can exceed these rates.

**By project:** Many contractors quote a flat fee for standard home exteriors, such as the **$260–$285 statewide averages**. Heavy grime or multiple stories increase the fee.

## Why Hire a Professional?

While renting a pressure washer may seem cheaper, improper use can cause siding or window damage. Professionals use commercial‑grade equipment and know the appropriate pressure and detergents for each surface. They also carry insurance and handle ladder work. Hiring a pro typically saves time and provides a safer, more thorough clean.
