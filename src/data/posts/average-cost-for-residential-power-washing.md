## What Affects the Cost of Residential Power Washing?

There is no flat rate for quality exterior cleaning. Pricing is influenced by:
### 1. Square Footage

Larger homes and driveways require more time, water, and cleaning solution.
### 2. Surface Type

Concrete, brick, vinyl, stucco, wood, and roofing materials all require different methods and pressure levels.
### 3. Level of Buildup

Heavy algae, mold, oil stains, and moss increase labor and chemical usage.
### 4. Cleaning Method Required

**Pressure washing:** concrete, brick, hard surfaces

**Soft washing:** siding, roofs, delicate materials

Using the wrong method can cause permanent damage — which is why pricing reflects expertise.
### 5. Accessibility & Safety

Steep roofs, multi-story homes, and tight access areas require additional safety measures and equipment.

## Why Cheap Power Washing Can Cost You More

Low pricing often means:

Excessive pressure used on siding

Water forced behind siding or under shingles

Etched concrete

Damaged paint or sealants

No insurance coverage

Fixing damage from improper washing can cost **far more than hiring a professional from the start**.

## Pressure Washing vs. Power Washing: Is There a Price Difference?

Most homeowners use the terms interchangeably. In practice:

**Pressure washing** uses high-pressure water

**Power washing** may include heated water

The **method used matters less than the technician’s knowledge**. Safe, effective cleaning is based on surface type — not just pressure.

## How Often Should Residential Power Washing Be Done?

Recommended maintenance:

**House washing:** Every 1–2 years

**Concrete:** Annually

**Roof Cleaning:** Every 2–3 years

Regular maintenance lowers long-term costs by preventing permanent staining and surface breakdown.
