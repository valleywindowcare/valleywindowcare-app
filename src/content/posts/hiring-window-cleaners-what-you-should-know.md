### Ask the Right Questions Before You Hire

While it’s natural to ask about cost, price alone won’t tell you whether a window cleaning company is professional, safe, or reliable. Here’s what else to ask:

#### ✅ Are They Licensed and Insured?

A professional company should carry both a valid business license and liability insurance. If a company isn’t able or willing to provide proof of these, that’s a red flag. Insurance is especially important—if an accident occurs on your property and the company isn’t covered, you could be held liable.

#### ✅ What Services Do They Offer?

Many companies go beyond window cleaning. Ask if they also offer services like screen or track cleaning, Gutter Cleaning, or pressure washing. Hiring a full-service provider can help you check off multiple to-dos with one call.

#### ✅ How Much Experience Do They Have?

A newer company may offer competitive pricing, but experience matters. Companies that have been in business for years tend to have established systems, trained teams, and a track record of customer satisfaction.

#### ✅ What Do Customers Say?

Online reviews can give you a clear sense of the company’s reputation. Look for consistent praise in areas like punctuality, professionalism, and results. You can also ask the company for referrals or testimonials from past clients—especially in your area.

#### ✅ Do They Screen Their Employees?

Your home is your safe space. Make sure the people entering it are properly vetted. Ask if employees undergo background checks and whether the company has any employee standards in place for professionalism and conduct.

#### ✅ Do They Provide Safety Training?

Window cleaning can involve working at heights or on uneven terrain. It’s important to hire a company that prioritizes safety. Ask whether their technicians are trained in ladder safety and fall prevention. A good company will be glad to share how they protect their workers and your property.

#### ✅ Are They Flexible with Scheduling?

Popular companies may book up quickly, but they should still be willing to work with you. Look for a team that communicates clearly and makes an effort to accommodate your schedule.
