## How to Clean Hard-to-Reach Windows

High or awkwardly placed windows don’t have to be a hassle. With the right gear—like a telescoping pole and an attachable scrubber—you can safely clean most second-story windows from the ground.
If a ladder is required, always follow these precautions:

Never climb higher than recommended

Avoid overreaching

Work with a partner for added safety

For some windows, especially on larger homes or commercial buildings, hiring professionals may be the safest and most efficient option.

## 5 Simple Steps to Clean Outdoor Windows

Here’s a step-by-step process to get your windows sparkling clean—without streaks, stress, or risk.
### 1. Spray the Windows

Begin by rinsing off loose dirt and debris using a garden hose with a spray nozzle. This removes surface-level grime and preps the glass for scrubbing.
### 2. Soak Your Sponge or Brush

Fill a bucket with warm water and a mild cleaning solution. Dish soap or a white vinegar mix works well. Submerge your sponge or brush, then wring out excess water so it’s damp but not dripping.
### 3. Scrub the Glass

Gently scrub each window using circular motions, starting from the top and working downward. For hard-to-reach areas, use your extension pole to maintain control and coverage.
### 4. Rinse Thoroughly

Use your hose to rinse off all soap residue. Pay close attention to corners and edges to avoid streaks or leftover dirt.
### 5. Dry for a Streak-Free Finish

Finish with a squeegee or microfiber cloth. Start at the top and pull downward in straight lines, wiping the blade after each pass. Alternatively, crumpled newspaper can also deliver a clear, polished result.

## Smart Window Cleaning Tips

**Clean top to bottom**: This prevents dirty water from dripping onto clean areas.

**Use two buckets**: One for soapy water, the other for rinsing your sponge or brush.

**Avoid sunny days**: Direct sun dries soap too fast, causing streaks.

**Don’t skip regular cleanings**: Clean windows 2–3 times a year to prevent heavy buildup.

**Use microfiber or newspaper**: Paper towels and regular cloths often leave lint or residue.

## FAQs: Window Cleaning Made Simple

**What’s the best way to avoid streaks?**Use a squeegee, dry from top to bottom, and clean with mild soap or a vinegar-water mix.
**How often should I clean my outside windows?**Generally, every 4–6 months. More often if you live near trees, pollen, or heavy traffic areas.
**Is dish soap safe for exterior windows?**Yes—mild dish soaps like Dawn effectively cut through grime without damaging glass or frames.
**Can I use paper towels?**It’s better to use microfiber cloths or newspaper to avoid lint and streaks.
**What do the pros use?**Professionals use squeegees, extension poles, commercial-grade cleaners, and purified water systems for the clearest finish possible.
