## How to Measure for Inside Mount Blinds

**Inside-mounted blinds** sit neatly within the window frame, offering a clean, built-in look. To measure properly:

**Measure the width** of the window at three points: the top, middle, and bottom. Use the narrowest measurement to ensure the blinds fit.

**Measure the height** from top to bottom on the left, center, and right sides of the window. Again, use the smallest number.

These measurements should be taken from the **inside edge of the trim**, where the blind will be recessed. If you&#8217;re ordering custom blinds, the manufacturer will make minor adjustments for brackets—no need to subtract on your own.

## How to Measure for Outside Mount Blinds

**Outside-mounted blinds** are installed on the wall above or around the window trim. This option is great for increasing light control and making windows appear larger.
To measure:

**Start with the headrail location**—this is the horizontal bar that holds the blinds. Mount it **1½ to 3 inches above the window** for best coverage.

**Measure the width** of the window and **add at least 1½ inches of overlap** to each side. For example, if your window is 25 inches wide, order blinds around 28 inches wide.

**Measure the height** from the headrail location to the bottom of the sill. If there is no sill, add another 1½ inches for full coverage.

This mounting style may cost slightly more due to added material, but it offers better privacy and light-blocking.

## Are Blinds Standard Sizes?

There are some “standard” window sizes, but they don’t always guarantee a perfect fit with off-the-shelf blinds. Knowing a few common dimensions can help:

**Picture windows** typically range from 2 to 8 feet wide, increasing in 1-foot intervals.

**Double-hung windows** are often between 36–72 inches wide and 24–48 inches tall.

However, your windows might still fall just outside of these ranges—making **custom blinds a smarter, more precise option**.

## Why Custom Blinds Are Worth It

While standard blinds may seem like a quicker option, they often come with limitations that custom blinds avoid:
🧵 **Better Fit** – Standard blinds may be off by an inch or more, resulting in poor coverage. Custom blinds are built to your exact measurements.
☀️ **Improved Performance** – Many custom blinds come lined with UV protection, insulating layers, or reflective backing for energy efficiency.
📏 **Smarter Design** – Standard blinds that are too long can be heavy and hard to operate. Custom blinds are balanced, lighter, and easier to use.
