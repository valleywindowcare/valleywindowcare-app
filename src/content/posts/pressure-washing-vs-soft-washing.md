### What Is Soft Washing?

Soft washing is a **low-pressure cleaning method** that combines water and eco-friendly cleaning solutions. Instead of blasting away dirt with force, soft washing gently treats and lifts contaminants like mold, mildew, algae, and grime. This method is ideal for delicate surfaces that might be damaged by high pressure.
#### Our Soft Washing Process Includes:

**Step 1: Pre-Wet the Surface**First, we soak the area with water to loosen surface dirt and prepare it for treatment.

**Step 2: Apply Cleaning Solution**Next, we spray a biodegradable solution that targets organic growth without harming your siding, plants, or finishes.

**Step 3: Dwell Time**After application, we let the solution sit for several minutes to break down unwanted buildup.

**Step 4: Gentle Rinse**We then rinse the surface using low pressure, ensuring all contaminants and residues are safely washed away.

**Step 5: Optional Post-Treatment**In some cases, we apply a second rinse to protect surfaces from future growth.

Thanks to this gentle approach, soft washing is perfect for materials like vinyl, painted wood, asphalt shingles, stucco, and composite decking.

### What Is Pressure Washing?

On the other hand, pressure washing uses **high-powered water jets** to remove tough grime, grease, and embedded debris from hard surfaces. While it’s much more forceful than soft washing, it’s also highly effective for surfaces that can handle the pressure.
#### How Pressure Washing Works:

**Step 1: Water Connection**We start by connecting a professional-grade pressure washer to your water supply.

**Step 2: Machine Setup**Based on the surface and condition, we adjust the PSI (pounds per square inch) and select the proper nozzle.

**Step 3: Surface Cleaning**Then, we spray the affected area with a controlled stream of high-pressure water, effectively blasting away dirt and buildup.

This method is ideal for driveways, sidewalks, patios, retaining walls, and other tough surfaces. However, it’s not recommended for more fragile materials like shingles or aged siding.

### Which Cleaning Method Is Right for You?

Deciding between soft washing and pressure washing comes down to your specific needs. If your surfaces are delicate or covered in organic growth like mold or algae, soft washing is the safer, more effective option. On the other hand, if you&#8217;re trying to remove thick grime or stains from durable materials, pressure washing may be the way to go.
